Primary measures: Wear a Mask

A mask is your first layer of protection againsts Covid-19. In most countries, wearing a masks is mandatory, so it is best to follow such guidelines. Masks minimise the amount of water vapour particles from leaving or entering through breathing. Since Covid-19 is spread through tiny water particles, a mask greatly reduces your chances of contracting it through such means.

It is advisable to keep your mask on 100% of the time. Only take it off if you are at home or when dining ONLY when the food has arrived.

It is really effective at limiting spread when everyone wears one, so play your part! Remember to dispose of masks properly, or wash them according to included instructions for the resuable ones.

Primary measures: Washing hands

Surfaces may have Covid-19 particles on them (along with other viruses or bacteria), so it is important to keep your hands clean, especially for the times where you have your mask down.

Hands should be washed after contacting any surfaces outside your household. It should be done before dining at a restaurant (if they are open) and when returning home. It is preferable if you can keep them clean as much as possible.

Soap dissolves the fat membrane of Viruses, including Covid-19 particles. It takes roughly 20 seconds for the soap to dissolve all viruses, so keep that in mind when washing your hands.

There are tips to making sure every corner of your hands are clean. Make sure to use enough soap to cover your hand and:

  1. Rub palms together
  2. Scrub between your fingers
  3. Rub the backs of your fingers
  4. Clean the thumbs
  5. Wash your fingertips + fingernauls
  6. Rub your wrists
It is courteous to dry your hands to prevent wetting the floor. With that, your hands are clean (for now)!

Additional Measure: Hand Sanitizer

Sometimes, the closest washroom is a little to far away, or you are unable to get there to begin with. That is when hand sanitizer is useful. Carrying a small bottle with you everywhere when you are outside would not hurt.

The hand sanitizer should be at least 60% alcohol, and be only used sparringly as a measure. Washing with soap is still far more effective after all, especially if your hands are visably dirty (e.g dirt, melted ice cream, oil fron fries). A good situation to use them would be like just before having a meal or after handling cash.

Additional Measure: Social Distancing

Covid-19 can be contracted through any sort of contact, which include being in close proximity to the infected. Socially distance can greaty help with limiting the spread.

Imagine a circle with a radius of about 1m, with you as the center. Being socially distance means staying away ~1m from others. That includes friend groups as well (unfortunately).

In places with seats, stay one seat apart from others.

You may be prompted to speak up, but avoiding talking in crowded spaces is recommended. Limiting group sizes to 4 people helps as well
